First talks with 2 Manipur insurgent groups held ||||
 The Centre and the Manipur government for the first time held a political dialogue with two Manipuri insurgent outfits, Kuki National Organisation (KNO) and United People's Front (UPF), on Wednesday. According to reports, the two groups jointly submitted a demand for 'statehood' to the Government of India. The groups said they sought 'self-determination' within the ambit of the Constitution. ||||

New Delhi, Jun 15 (PTI) Manipur's two insurgent outfits Kuki National Organisation (KNO) and United People's Front (UPF) today joined in parleys with the Centre to bring lasting peace in their area of influence.
The first round of talks was held here after the central government decided to hold tripartite talks between it, Manipur government and the two insurgent groups -- KNO and UPF.
The rebel groups indicated their demands in the presence of representatives of central and state governments, a Home Ministry official said.
