PM Modi plays drums with Tanzanian President ||||
 During the third leg of his four-nation Africa tour, Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Sunday joined Tanzanian President John Magufuli in playing traditional Tanzanian drums for nearly one minute. Following this, External Affairs Ministry spokesperson Vikas Swarup tweeted, "New beats to the #IndiaAfrica rhythm! Earlier, Modi had played the drums during an official visit to Japan in September 2014. ||||

PM Narendra Modi's visit to the African continent has thus far been quite well-received by the media across the world. Other than discussing serious political and business matters, it seems the Prime Minister isn't averse to having some fun while on the job.
Upon arriving in Tanzania on Sunday morning, Modi decided to join in with Tanzanian President John Magufuli and try his hands at playing a traditional Tanzania drums, and he did a swell job of it.

This is not the first time Modi has played the drums while on an official visit. Previously the Prime Minister played the ceremonial Japanese drums at the inauguration of the TCS Japan Technology and Cultural Academy, in Tokyo, Japan on September 2, 2014.
