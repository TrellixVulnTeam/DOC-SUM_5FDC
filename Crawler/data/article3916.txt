India shares intel info with 24 countries: Govt ||||
 Responding to a question in Lok Sabha, Minister of State for Home, Haribhai Chaudhary on Tuesday said that India is sharing intelligence information with 24 countries including China as part of Joint Working Groups on Counter-Terrorism. Chaudhary added that India also shares experience, expertise, good practices and issues of mutual concern with foreign nation members. ||||

India is sharing intelligence on terrorism with China and 23 other countries via the mechanism of Joint Working Groups on Counter-Terrorism, Lok Sabha on Tuesday was told.
In response to a question, Minister of State for Home, Haribhai Parthibhai Chaudhary, said, “India has Joint Working Groups on Counter-Terrorism with 24 countries, including China.”
“Meetings of these Joint Working Groups are being held from time to time to discuss counter-terrorism issues and share intelligence, experience, expertise, good practices and issues of mutual concern.”
The minister added that cross-border terrorism issues are raised by India in Joint Working Group forum with foreign countries.
He said, however, that further details in this regard “cannot be disclosed in the interest of national security and bilateral relations with foreign states”.
