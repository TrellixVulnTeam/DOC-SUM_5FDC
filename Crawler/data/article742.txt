No talk on military cooperation in Syria: Russia ||||
 A Russian spokesperson has denied reports suggesting US Secretary of State John Kerry and Russian President Vladimir Putin talked about military coordination in Syria. He said, "A lot of questions remain regarding real interaction in implementing operations in Syria." Earlier reports suggested that Kerry will propose sharing of military intelligence with Russia on al-Qaeda and ISIS. ||||

MOSCOW U.S. Secretary of State John Kerry did not directly discuss military cooperation between Moscow and Washington in Syria when he met Russian President Vladimir Putin last night, the Kremlin said on Friday.
"A lot of questions remain regarding real interaction in implementing operations in Syria," Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov told a conference call with journalists.
