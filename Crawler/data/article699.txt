The Crime Branch police of Kheda district have busted an inter-state gang wanted in several cases of ATM fraud in Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh and Gujarat.
The police have also recovered Rs 3.92 lakh cash and several credit cards from the four arrested accused belonging to Haryana.

According to the police, the gang swindled unsuspecting persons from Kapadvanj, Una, Ahmedabad, Nadiad, Petlad and other parts of the state.
The gang would visit Gujarat in different cars and study the ATM location of banks with help of a Hindi Atlas.
“The operation would last five days, in which, they would lure unsuspecting elderly persons or gullible users to share their sensitive financial information and swindle them. We suspect they have committed a fraud of over Rs 20 lakh. The probe is on to trace the owners of the cards recovered,” said Police Inspector VJ Rathod.
Kheda SP Maninder Partapsinh Pawar has ordered the department to recover the money swindled by the men and trace the entire organized racket that could have its roots in Delhi.
