'Hrithik signs 550 cr deal for rights to films' ||||
 As per reports, actor Hrithik Roshan has signed a deal worth 550 crore for the satellite rights to his next six films. The 42-year-old actor will be seen next in Ashutosh Gowariker's 'Mohenjo Daro', which releases on August 12. Meanwhile, Hrithik portrays a blind man in 'Kaabil', which will release on January 26, 2017, along with Shah Rukh Khan's 'Raees'. ||||

According to reports in a city daily, Bollywood actor Hrithik Roshan has cracked a 550-crore deal for the satellite rights of his next 6 films. The actor was last seen in Siddharth Anand's Bang Bang, which grossed a massive Rs 340 crores worldwide.

The deal was clinched keeping in mind Hrithik's films since 2011's Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara, 2012's Agneepath, 2013's Krrish 3 and Bang Bang in 2014 - all of which grossed the benchmark of a 100 crores each. This deal is only second of its kind, after a similar deal was signed between Salman Khan and a leading TV network for Rs 500 crores for his films between 2013 and 2017.
The Krrish actor will be next seen in Ashutosh Gowariker's historical epic Mohenjo Daro and then in Sanjay Gupta's Kaabil. It is reported that for Kaabil, he will be essaying the role of a blind man.
There were unconfirmed reports recently that even Varun Dhawan has entered a 300-crore deal for his upcoming films barring Judwaa 2.
