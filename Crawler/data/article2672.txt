Redmi Note 3 phone to launch in India on March 3 ||||
 Xiaomi will launch its Redmi Note 3 smartphone in India on March 3. The dual-SIM phone features a fingerprint scanner, a 5.5-inch full HD display, a 13 MP camera, a 4,000 mAh battery and is powered by the MediaTek Helio X10 octa-core processor. The ultra-thin, 4G-enabled phone will be available in two storage variants of 16 GB and 32 GB. ||||

Xiaomi Redmi Note 3 is launching in India on March 3, confirmed the company on Twitter. Xiaomi’s Manu Jain had earlier told IndianExpress.com that the company was working hard to bring its new devices to India. He had confirmed that the Redmi Note 3 and Mi 5 (yet to revealed) will be coming to India. Xiaomi had also announced a Explorer contest for 100 lucky winners in India.
The Redmi Note 3 will be the first ‘new’ phone from the company in the country. The last so called new phone from Xiaomi for India was Mi 4i, although it also introduced upgraded and made-in-India phones like Redmi 2 Prime and Redmi Note 4G Prime in 2015.


Xiaomi Redmi Note 3 was recently launched in China and comes with a full metal body, and a fingerprint scanner on the back.
The Redmi Note 3 has a 5.5-inch full HD display, runs the Qualcomm Snapdragon 650 processor, and sports a 13 mp rear camera with a 5 mp front camera. Xiaomi has launched two versions of the phone. One comes with 2GB RAM and 16GB storage space and the second has 3GB RAM and 32GB storage space.
The Redmi Note 3 runs Xiaomi’s MIUI 7 OS on top of Android Lollipop and has an extra large 4000 mAh battery. Like other Xiaomi smartphones, this is a 4G ready Dual SIM phone with both slots supporting 4G. Xiaomi says the phone is ultra-thin at 8.65 mm and weighs 164g.
