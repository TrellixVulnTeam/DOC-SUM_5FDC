Militancy must be dealt firmly in Kashmir: Sonia ||||
 Amid unrest in the Valley, Congress President Sonia Gandhi on Wednesday said militancy must be dealt firmly in Kashmir as recent events are tragic, which pose a grave challenge to the country. The Centre must also remember the distinction between civilians, separatists and terrorists, she added. The death toll in the Valley has reached 42 with nearly 3,000 people injured. ||||
 The Indian Express ||||

Saying that the recent violence in Kashmir was tragic, Congress President Sonia Gandhi Wednesday said militancy posed a grave danger to the country and it must be dealt with firmly.
At the same time, she added, that “we must introspect as to what has driven scores of youth to such levels of anger and protest”.
The violence in Kashmir has so far claimed the lives of 43 people. Opposition parties in the Parliament demanded the government to constitute an all-party delegation to visit Kashmir and engage with all the stake-holder, including separatists.
Union Home Minister Rajnath Singh said the violence in Kashmir was sponsored by Pakistan and added that they should stop worrying about Muslims living in India.
