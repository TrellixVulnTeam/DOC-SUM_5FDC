Russia can overthrow human rights court verdict ||||
 Russian President Vladimir Putin signed a new law that allows the country's Constitutional Court to deliberate and ignore verdicts by international human rights courts. Under this law published on Tuesday, High Courts can decide if they should enforce decisions of the European Court of Human Rights (ECHR). This decision was taken after an ECHR ruling against mass surveillance in Russia. ||||

Russian President Vladimir Putin has signed a law allowing the Constitutional Court of Russia to decide whether to fulfil or not the decisions of international human rights courts.
The law, published on Tuesday on the official government portal of legislative information, enables the Russian high court to overthrow decisions of the Strasbourg-based European Court of Human Rights
