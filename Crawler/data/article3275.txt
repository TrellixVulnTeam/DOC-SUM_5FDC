Govt to float tenders for three new ports ||||
 The government will float tenders for three new ports in the states of Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, and West Bengal by March, this year, Union Shipping and Transport Minister Nitin Gadkari said on Wednesday. These projects require investments between 18,000 crore and 20,000 crore, Gadkari added. The work on these projects will begin by April-May, he said. ||||
 The Indian Express ||||

Union Shipping and Transport Minister Nitin Gadkari on Wednesday said the government will float tenders for three new major ports in Maharashtra, West Bengal and Tamil Nadu by March, entailing investment of Rs 20,000 crore.
“I can assure you that we will issue tenders for these three projects which require an investment of Rs 18,000-20,000 crore, by March and the work on the projects like breakwaters will begin by April-May,” Gadkari said. The three ports will come up in Wadhwan (Maharashtra), Sagar (West Bengal) and Colachel (Tamil Nadu).
