Panama Papers stakeholders names released ||||
 The International Consortium of Investigative Journalists on Monday released the details of around 200 offshore companies found in the 'Panama Papers' leaked from law firm Mossack Fonseca. The 'structured data', available for download, contains details of shareholders, beneficiaries, intermediaries and addresses. However, ICIJ will not release the 11.5 million files leaked from Fonseca's database. ||||
 The Indian Express ||||

The International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ), with which The Indian Express has partnered in the Panama Papers investigation, has Monday released the names of shareholders, beneficiaries, intermediaries and addresses pertaining to over 200,000 offshore entities found in Mossack Fonseca’s internal database.
Also called the ‘structured data’, these are names for which identity proofs and addresses are available in the Mossack Fonseca documents.
Click here to access the database (data is searchable). 
ICIJ will not release any of the 11.5 million files found in the internal database of the Panamanian corporate service provider Mossack Fonseca. The structured data will be available for download.
The Indian Express has already reported on a large number of Indian names in the data as part of its ongoing investigations. The structured data is in addition to more than 100,000 offshore companies already released in June 2013 from the Offshore Leaks data set.
